# Marketing claim sheet — fill in before any campaign

This is the document that controls every word of your marketing copy. Until it's filled in, you have no business writing landing-page headlines, Twitter posts, or sales emails.

The reason this matters: if you launch with a vague pitch ("we find deals"), nobody bites. If you launch with a specific, falsifiable claim ("we find $50+ margin auction-ending deals on 2024 NFL rookies under $100"), the right audience self-selects in 3 seconds.

## Section 1 — Audience


Test: someone reading that sentence should immediately know whether they ARE that person.

**Bad example:** "Sports card collectors and flippers."
**Better:** "NFL flippers who already check eBay 5+ times a day for under-market rookies."
**Best:** "NFL/NBA flippers running 10-50 cards per week who use eBay's "ending soon" filter manually and routinely lose deals because they were 30 seconds too late."

(The "best" version names the audience, the volume tier, the existing behavior, and the pain point. Use that level of specificity.)

## Section 2 — The single claim

One sentence that says what SnipeWins does *for that audience*. Must be falsifiable — i.e., a user could verify it after one week of using the product.

```
[FILL IN]
```

**Bad example:** "We help you find deals."
**Better:** "We surface auction-ending sports card deals at 30%+ under market."
**Best:** "We tell you exactly how high to bid on each ending auction — the max price where you still profit when you flip."

Notice the "best" version is concrete enough that a user can verify it. After one win, they know whether the claim was true.

Each hook must be ≤ 8 words. Each must promise something the product actually delivers (not aspirational).

Example structure:
- Hook 1 (the hero): one short sentence that states the outcome the user wants.
- Hook 2 (the proof): a number or specific detail that makes hook 1 believable.
- Hook 3 (the urgency): why now, not later.

Examples to seed your thinking:

## Section 6 — Channels

Where will you actually post?

For each: name the specific subreddit/Discord/Twitter list/Facebook group, plus an existing relationship there (you've posted before, you're a member, etc.). DO NOT list channels you've never used.

```
1. [Channel + relationship]
2. [Channel + relationship]
3. [Channel + relationship]
```

If you can't list 3 channels with existing relationships, your launch is going to be quiet. Build the relationships before launch.

## Section 7 — Forbidden phrases

Marketing copy that always rings false. Add to this list whenever you catch yourself drafting one of these:

- "Game-changing"
- "Revolutionary"
- "AI-powered" (unless you specifically use AI in a measurable way and disclose how)
- "Disrupting the [X] industry"
- "Built by collectors for collectors" (unless literally true)
- "The only [X] that [Y]" (very rarely true; lawyers care)
- Anything in italics in this list

```

```

Acceptable proofs:
- A demo video showing one real win
- A scan log with dated and verifiable winning bid amounts
- A user testimonial from a closed-alpha user who agreed in writing to be quoted
- A specific number like "engine surfaces 80-150 deals per peak-hour scan" with reference to the trace data

Unacceptable proofs:
- Fabricated user names or testimonials (this was the LiveDealFeed problem)
- "Industry experts agree…"
- Numbers without methodology

## How to use this doc

1. Fill in Sections 1-8. Don't skip any. Don't move on with placeholders.
2. Read back the full doc as a paragraph. Does it sound like a product you'd buy?
3. Send it to one friend in your target audience. One question: *"Would you click this?"* If no, rewrite. Don't move past this until yes.
4. Now — and only now — write your landing page copy from this doc. Every headline, subhead, button label, and FAQ answer must trace back to a sentence in here.

When in doubt, cut. Marketing copy is too long 95% of the time.
